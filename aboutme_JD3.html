About Me

Researcher | Psychologist | Aging Expert

My Story

I am a reader, cycling enthusist, aging expert and data-driven researcher.

My journey into quantitative research began at the Max Planck Institute for Human Development in Berlin. There, I studied developmental pathways using long-term longitudinal data to explore the dynamic interplay between cognition, health, and environmental factors. This experience deepened my passion for solving complex problems with computational tools.

I have been co-leading a significant ten-year longitudinal study, which has provided me with invaluable management experience and the responsibility of working with highly sensitive data.

Now, as a quantitative research scientist at Amazon, I continue to bridge the gap between rigorous data analysis and innovative thinking, driving impactful business solutions.

I also serve as a consultant in my areas of expertise, including work with the European Commission, as it allows me to give back, share knowledge, and continually learn from diverse perspectives.

Along my journey, I have learned advanced statistics, principles of machine learning, coding, survey design, and data management through both classical training and hands on experience. However, as a researcher with a background in psychology, I value the narratives that data helps us uncover. I don't stop at the numbers alone; this approach allows me to holistically pursue my work. For me, blending quantitative and qualitative insights underscores the importance of integrative technology that feature diverse voices.
